AI Product Engineering Academy

A 75-day, 750-hour venture-building program where students use AI as a coworker across thinking, field research, hardware, manufacturing, operations, minimum useful software, communication, finance, and execution.

Core promise

Turn students into AI-powered real-world problem solvers.

Students should learn to identify meaningful problems, understand physical and business context, assemble practical solutions, work in teams, communicate with customers, and launch viable businesses.

Detailed phase Days 1-6 foundation sprint Planning tracker Direction correction todo Student action Founding batch application

Design doctrine

Problem first. Reality first. AI as accelerator.

  1. Real problem first
  2. Reality and context second
  3. Practical solution design third
  4. AI as accelerator
  5. Software as glue, interface, data layer, or automation layer
  6. Business model as delivery system

Software is important, but it is not the default output. The scarce skill is understanding messy real-world problems and deciding what combination of hardware, process, people, data, software, AI, manufacturing, and business model can actually solve them.

Avoid and produce

The course should reject shallow AI/software outputs.

Avoid

  • Generic SaaS apps
  • Clone apps and todo apps
  • Chatbots without operational value
  • Dashboards without real data
  • AI wrappers that do not change a real workflow
  • Startup ideas that exist only as pitch decks

Produce

  • Factory workflow improvements
  • Inspection, sorting, tracking, or monitoring systems
  • Low-cost hardware jigs or tools
  • Quality control or maintenance processes
  • Local business order, inventory, support, or coordination systems
  • Hybrid hardware, operations, software, and business solutions

Candidate selection

Do not select only by marks.

The right student is curious, coachable, willing to work hard, and comfortable with ambiguity.

Selection funnel

  1. Application form
  2. AI awareness and learning attitude test
  3. 2-hour problem-solving challenge
  4. Group discussion
  5. Short interview
  6. Parent/student orientation

Selection criteria

  • Curiosity and learning speed
  • Willingness to build daily
  • Basic English and communication potential
  • Team behavior
  • Practical problem awareness
  • Minimum laptop/internet readiness

Operating rhythm

The day type changes by phase.

A fixed routine for all 75 days would become fake. Early days need mindset repair, middle days need field and build work, and later days need launch pressure.

FoundationRepair thinking, learning habits, and AI verification.
Skill sprintTeach one practical capability and submit an artifact.
FieldworkInterviews, observations, photos, notes, and problem mapping.
BuildCreate or improve a solution with visible contribution checks.
Reality testCustomer feedback, process trial, data collection, and cost check.
ReviewDemo, critique, AI report, mentor clinic, and next-week planning.
BusinessPricing, delivery model, legal/accounting basics, and sales material.

75-day structure

Phases and outputs.

Days 1-6

Awakening and baseline

Personal AI workbench, baseline profile, observation notebook, and first tiny useful artifact.

Open details
Days 7-12

Thinking like a builder

Problem framing, assumptions, constraints, estimation, tradeoffs, and real-world case breakdowns.

Days 13-18

AI coworker mastery

Delegation, verification, research workflows, technical planning, and hallucination detection.

Days 19-30

Digital tools for real-world work

Data capture, forms, dashboards, automations, and interfaces tied to field problems.

Days 31-42

Hardware and manufacturing

CAD, BOM, sensors, basic firmware, process selection, costing, and supplier research.

Days 43-48

Real customer discovery

Local business interviews, painful problem discovery, willingness-to-pay checks, venture briefs.

Days 49-60

Venture build 1

Solution prototypes, weekly demos, pricing, first outreach campaign, and mentor reviews.

Days 61-72

Venture build 2

Customer-tested iteration, revenue experiment, launch plan, and incorporation readiness.

Days 73-75

Demo and incorporation

Final demo day, jury review, prizes, incorporation sprint, and 90-day incubation plan.
