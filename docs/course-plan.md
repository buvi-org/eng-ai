# AI Product Engineering Academy

## Core Promise

Turn engineering students into AI-powered product builders who can identify real problems, build practical solutions, work in teams, communicate with customers, and launch viable businesses.

The course is not a generic AI tools workshop. It is a 75-day, 750-hour venture-building program where students use AI as a real coworker across thinking, software, hardware, manufacturing, communication, finance, and execution.

## Business Model

### Founding Cohort

- Fee: Rs 30,000 per student
- Batch size: 30 students
- Revenue per batch: Rs 9,00,000
- Ideal launch: 3 to 4 parallel or staggered batches
- Goal: collect proof, testimonials, project outcomes, college partnerships, and placement/startup stories

### Standard Cohort

- Fee: Rs 50,000 per student
- Batch size: 30 students
- Revenue per batch: Rs 15,00,000
- Four batches: Rs 60,00,000 gross revenue
- Positioning: practical AI product engineering with real prototypes, customer work, and business launch support

### Premium Cohort

- Fee: Rs 75,000 to Rs 1,00,000 per student
- Includes deeper mentorship, factory access, legal incorporation support, investor demo day, stronger industry projects, and post-course incubation

### Add-On Revenue

- College licensing model
- Corporate problem statements and sponsored challenges
- Startup incubation equity or success fee
- Weekend workshops for non-cohort students
- Alumni membership and advanced labs
- Consulting projects delivered by supervised student teams

## Candidate Selection

The course should not admit students only by marks. The right student is curious, coachable, willing to work hard, and comfortable with ambiguity.

### Selection Funnel

1. Application form
2. AI awareness and learning attitude test
3. 2-hour problem-solving challenge
4. Group discussion
5. Short interview
6. Parent/student orientation for commitment clarity

### Selection Criteria

- Curiosity and learning speed
- Willingness to build daily
- Basic English and communication potential
- Team behavior
- Practical problem awareness
- Ability to handle feedback
- Minimum laptop/internet readiness

### Cohort Composition

- 30 students per batch
- 6 venture teams of 5 students
- Each team should mix software, mechanical/electrical, operations, communication, and business interest
- Every student owns an individual role and weekly measurable output

## Course Duration

- Total length: 75 training days exactly
- Total effort: 10 hours per day
- Weekly cadence: 6 days per week
- Total hours: 750 hours
- Shape: 12 full weeks plus 3 final demo/incubation days

The course itself should be designed, monitored, and optimized by AI.

### Student Data Captured Daily

- Attendance and punctuality
- Daily build logs
- GitHub commits or artifact uploads
- AI conversation summaries
- Customer interview notes
- Team contribution scores
- Mentor observations
- Quiz/challenge scores
- Demo quality
- Blockers and confidence level

### AI Feedback Loops

- Daily individual progress summary
- Weekly team health report
- Skill-gap detection
- Suggested mentor interventions
- Personalized homework
- Project risk alerts
- Hiring/startup readiness score
- Cohort-level curriculum adjustments

### Human Oversight

AI can monitor and recommend, but mentors make final decisions. The goal is individual attention at scale, not automated judgment without context.

## 75-Day Structure

### Phase 1: Awakening and Baseline - Days 1-6

- Understand the AI wave and why college learning is insufficient
- Baseline tests for thinking, communication, AI use, and execution
- Set up tools: laptop, GitHub, AI workspace, notes, task board
- Ship first tiny artifact in 24 hours

Output: personal AI workbench, baseline profile, first shipped mini-project.

### Phase 2: Thinking Like a Builder - Days 7-12

- Problem framing
- First principles
- Assumptions and constraints
- Estimation and tradeoffs
- Engineering economics
- Daily real-world case breakdowns

Output: 10 problem breakdowns and one local problem selected for deeper exploration.

### Phase 3: AI Coworker Mastery - Days 13-18

- AI delegation
- Verification
- Research workflows
- Product specification with AI
- Technical planning with AI
- AI limitations and hallucination detection

Output: AI workflow playbook and verified research dossier.

### Phase 4: Software Sprint - Days 19-30

- Build and deploy a practical web app
- Database, auth, APIs, deployment, testing
- Each student contributes visible commits
- AI helps generate, explain, test, and refactor

Output: deployed software product v1.

### Phase 5: Hardware and Manufacturing Sprint - Days 31-42

- Product design
- CAD, BOM, sensors, basic firmware
- Manufacturing process selection
- Cost estimation
- Supplier research

Output: prototype design package with CAD, BOM, cost sheet, and build plan.

### Phase 6: Real Customer Discovery - Days 43-48

- Interview local businesses, factories, shops, colleges, and professionals
- Identify painful repetitive problems
- Validate willingness to pay
- Convert ideas into venture briefs

Output: 20 customer conversations per team and shortlisted business idea.

### Phase 7: Venture Build 1 - Days 49-60

- Teams build MVPs
- Weekly public demos
- Pricing and landing page
- First outreach campaign
- Technical and business mentorship

Output: working MVP, website, demo video, first customer feedback.

### Phase 8: Venture Build 2 - Days 61-72

- Improve product based on feedback
- Add payment/ordering/lead capture where relevant
- Prepare incorporation documents for top teams
- Build operating plan, costing, and launch plan

Output: launch-ready venture package.

### Phase 9: Demo, Incorporation, and Incubation - Days 73-75

- Final demo day
- Investor/industry panel
- Prize awards
- Incorporation of top 2 to 3 businesses
- 90-day post-course incubation plan

Output: 2 to 3 incorporated ventures, cohort portfolio, alumni roadmap.

## Venture Formation

Each cohort should create 6 venture teams. Every team builds a business candidate, but only the strongest 2 to 3 should be incorporated by the end of the course.

### Incorporation Readiness Checklist

- Real customer problem
- Clear buyer
- MVP or service delivery process
- Pricing model
- Basic website and lead capture
- Founding team roles
- Unit economics
- First customer conversations
- 90-day plan
- Legal and compliance readiness

## Prize Model

Create a visible prize pool to make the course exciting and serious.

- Best Venture: Rs 2,00,000 grant or tool/equipment credits
- Best Real Customer Impact: Rs 1,00,000
- Best Technical Build: Rs 75,000
- Best Individual Builder: Rs 50,000
- Demo Day Audience Choice: Rs 25,000
- Incubation support: workspace, mentorship, supplier access, cloud credits, legal/accounting help

Prizes should reward evidence, not showmanship: customer proof, execution speed, technical quality, business clarity, and team behavior.
